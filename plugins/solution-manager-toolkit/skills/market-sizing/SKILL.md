# Market Sizing

Domain knowledge for sizing market opportunities with top-down and bottom-up approaches.

## When to Use

- Framing the scale of a problem or opportunity during brainstorm
- Building a business case for a new capability or product
- Evaluating addressable market for investment management solutions

## Approaches

**Top-Down (TAM → SAM → SOM):**
- **TAM** (Total Addressable Market): Total AUM or revenue pool for the asset class/strategy type
- **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market): Subset reachable by your platform (channel, minimums, geography)
- **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market): Realistic capture based on competitive position and capacity

**Bottom-Up (Unit Economics):**
- Number of target clients × average AUM per client × fee rate (bps)
- Account minimums and capacity constraints
- Wallet share: what % of a client's total AUM could you manage?

## Investment Management Defaults

- Fee rates: Separate accounts 20-50 bps, direct indexing 15-35 bps, ETF wraps 5-15 bps
- Channels: Institutional (pension, endowment, OCIO), RIA/wealth, retail (wrap programs)
- Gatekeepers: Investment consultants, home office due diligence, platform inclusion
- Growth factors: Tax-loss harvesting demand, ESG/custom screening adoption, fee compression migration from active

## Key Principles

- Always present both top-down and bottom-up — they should roughly converge.
- TAM without SAM is vanity. SAM without SOM is optimism. Size all three.
- For investment management, AUM is the primary unit, not users or seats.
- Fee compression is real — size the market at today's fee rates, not yesterday's.
- Consultant/OCIO gatekeepers control institutional access — factor their adoption timeline.
