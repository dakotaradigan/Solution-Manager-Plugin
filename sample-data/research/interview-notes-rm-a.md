# Interview Notes: Relationship Manager A — Senior Relationship Manager, Wealth Advisory

**Date:** 2026-02-15
**Interviewer:** Dakota Radigan
**Duration:** 45 min
**Format:** 1:1 video call

## Context
Participant manages ~40 client relationships totaling $1.2B AUM. 10 years at the firm. Primary interface between clients/prospects and the investment team.

## Raw Notes

- Loses 2-3 prospects per quarter because transition proposals take too long: "By the time we get the analysis back, they've already signed with someone else"
- Current process: emails the transition team a forwarded message from the prospect, attaches whatever holdings statement the client provided (sometimes PDF, sometimes CSV, sometimes a photo of a screen)
- "I don't know what format to send things in. Every time I ask, I get a different answer depending on who I talk to"
- Has no visibility into where a transition request is in the pipeline — "I just ping Raj on Slack every few days and ask if it's done"
- Clients increasingly expect interactive proposals: "One prospect told me their other manager showed them a tool where they could drag a slider for tax budget and see the tracking error change. We sent a 15-page PDF."
- Wants to be able to show clients a rough scenario in the first meeting, even if it's not final: "I don't need exact numbers, I need a conversation starter"
- Frustrated by back-and-forth: "I submit a request, get asked for the tax ID. Then the cost basis. Then the account type. Why can't we just have a form?"
- Mentions that the firm's biggest competitor turns around transition proposals in 48 hours
- Multi-account households are the worst: "When a client has taxable, IRA, and a trust, I have to submit three separate requests and hope someone on the other end realizes they're related"
- "The proposals we send look like they were made in 2005. Competitors are sending branded, interactive reports."

## Key Quotes
> "I'm a salesperson. Every day without a proposal is a day the prospect is talking to someone else."

> "The last prospect I lost — $35M account — told me our competitor had their proposal ready before our team even acknowledged my email."

> "I shouldn't need to understand tax lots to submit a transition request. That's not my job. My job is to close the client."

## Observed Pain Points
1. Proposal turnaround too slow (weeks, not days)
2. No standardized intake — requests via email/Slack with inconsistent information
3. Zero visibility into request status
4. No ability to show rough scenarios in initial meetings
5. Static PDF output vs. interactive competitor proposals
6. Multi-account households treated as separate requests
7. Repeated back-and-forth for missing information
