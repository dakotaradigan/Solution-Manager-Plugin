## Open-Ended Responses — Key Themes

### "What's the #1 thing you'd fix?" (Top responses by frequency)
1. Faster proposal turnaround (13 mentions)
2. Standardized intake process / form (9 mentions)
3. Interactive proposals instead of static PDF (7 mentions)
4. Visibility into request status (6 mentions)
5. Automated tax lot analysis (5 mentions)
6. Multi-account household handling (4 mentions)
7. Pre-trade compliance checks during modeling (3 mentions)

### Selected Verbatims

**Relationship Managers:**
> "I lost a $35M prospect last month because our proposal took three weeks. Their other manager had something ready in two days."

> "I have no idea where my requests are. I submit an email and pray. Then I Slack Raj, who's drowning."

**Transition Analysts:**
> "I spend the first day of every transition just getting the data into a usable format. If we had a standard intake form with the right fields, I'd get that day back."

> "My biggest fear is the Excel file corrupting. There's no backup, no version control. It's a 47-tab workbook that runs the entire operation."

**Portfolio Managers:**
> "I need to see tax impact and tracking error on the same chart. Right now I get a tax number in one tab and a tracking error number in another and I'm supposed to just trust they're consistent."

> "We've done hundreds of transitions but we have zero data on how they actually performed. Did our projected tracking error match reality? No idea."

**Compliance:**
> "We review the proposal after it's been built. If there's a restriction violation — a do-not-buy, a sector cap, a concentrated stock rule — the whole thing gets reworked. That wastes everyone's time."

> "Wash sale monitoring across households is a regulatory risk. We're relying on one analyst's manual Excel process to keep us compliant."

**Technology:**
> "There's no API, no system of record, no event trail. Transitions happen in email and Excel. We can't build automation on top of that."
