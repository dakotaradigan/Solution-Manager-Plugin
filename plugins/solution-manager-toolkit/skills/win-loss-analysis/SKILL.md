# Win/Loss Analysis

Domain knowledge for structured post-decision analysis of competitive outcomes.

## When to Use

- Post-RFP debrief (won or lost)
- Platform selection decision retrospective
- Competitive loss analysis for product strategy
- Quarterly competitive landscape review or analyzing patterns across recent RFP outcomes

## Analysis Framework

For each win/loss, capture:
- **Decision:** What was decided and by whom (client, consultant, investment committee, OCIO)
- **Outcome:** Won / Lost / No decision / Incumbent retained
- **Decision Factors:** Rank the top 3-5 factors that drove the outcome
- **Our Position:** How we scored on each factor (strong / neutral / weak)
- **Competitor Position:** How the winner/alternative scored (if known)
- **What Worked:** Capabilities, relationships, or positioning that helped
- **What Didn't:** Gaps, misperceptions, or execution failures
- **Actionable Insight:** What to change, build, or message differently

## Investment Management Decision Factors

- Performance track record and attribution quality
- Tax management capabilities (TLH, transition management, tax budgeting)
- Technology and reporting (portal, API access, custom reporting)
- Operational due diligence (ODD) readiness
- Fee competitiveness relative to strategy type
- Consultant/OCIO relationship and database ratings
- Customization flexibility (ESG screens, restrictions, model tilts)
- Transition management capabilities and cost estimates

## Key Principles

- Losses are more valuable than wins — they reveal gaps the market is pricing in.
- Interview the decision-maker when possible, not just your sales team's narrative.
- Track patterns across multiple wins/losses — single data points mislead.
- Separate "we lost on product" from "we lost on relationship/price" — different fixes.
- Feed findings back into roadmap priorities and competitive positioning.
